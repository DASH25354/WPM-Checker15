Three-minute Training

【14】Kenshi Yonezu is a Japanese singer-songwriter and visual artist. He was born on March 10, 1991. As a student, he made songs at night on his computer. He also drew simple storyboards for videos. He chose colors and camera moves so the music and images matched well. He started by making songs alone. He mixed pop, rock, and soft dance beats into clear hooks. His lyrics use simple words to show deep feelings: hope, pain, and the beauty of daily life. Many listeners say his songs help when they feel lost. He shared works online and found fans who loved both sound and art. In videos, he often designs the concept himself. Each project feels like a small world with its own light. On stage, he is calm and focused. Lights and screens add to the story. He speaks little and lets the music talk. New listeners start with famous songs and often press repeat. His careful layers reward close listening and make a strong, memorable experience.

Japanese bacon is usually sliced very thin, so it gets crispy fast. Western bacon is cured and smoked longer, and it is sliced thin. More water in bacon can make it steam and stay soft in the pan. Adding a lot of salt and sugar always makes any bacon crispy without heat.
